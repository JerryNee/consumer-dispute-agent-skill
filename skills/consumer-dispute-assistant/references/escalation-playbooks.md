# Escalation Playbooks

Use this reference to choose a practical route. Verify current official forms, deadlines, and laws before giving jurisdiction-specific instructions.

## Default Route

1. Merchant support: Send a concise factual request with evidence and requested resolution.
2. Merchant escalation: Ask for supervisor, billing team, trust/safety, executive support, or written final response.
3. Payment or platform dispute: Card issuer, bank, PayPal, app store, marketplace, booking platform, or telecom/utility complaint channel.
4. Public or regulator complaint draft: BBB, FTC, CFPB, state attorney general, public utility commission, transportation agency, insurance regulator, or local consumer protection office.
5. Legal-adjacent referral: Demand letter, small claims research, or attorney referral only if the user asks and the value/risk justifies it.

## Subscription Cancellation Failure

Best first outputs:

- Cancellation/refund request to merchant.
- Evidence table with signup date, cancellation date, charge date, and renewal terms.
- Payment dispute statement if merchant refuses or ignores.

Look for:

- Trial end date.
- Cancellation confirmation.
- App store or payment-platform subscription manager records.
- Prior support tickets.
- Whether service was used after cancellation.

Suggested route:

Merchant billing support -> app store/payment platform if billed there -> card issuer dispute -> FTC/state AG complaint draft for deceptive or hard-to-cancel patterns.

## Duplicate, Incorrect, or Unauthorized Charge

Best first outputs:

- Short billing correction request.
- Charge comparison table.
- Bank/card dispute statement.

Look for:

- Two charges with same merchant/date/amount.
- Failed checkout followed by successful checkout.
- Charge after account closure.
- Evidence that user did not authorize the transaction.

Suggested route:

Merchant billing support -> payment processor/platform -> card issuer/bank dispute. If identity theft is suspected, treat as high-stakes and advise official identity-theft resources after verification.

## Refund Denial or Product Not as Described

Best first outputs:

- Refund request citing facts, not legal threats.
- Return/refund policy snapshot.
- Defect or mismatch evidence list.

Look for:

- Product listing, order confirmation, delivery date.
- Photos/video of issue.
- Return attempt and merchant response.
- Warranty or satisfaction guarantee language.

Suggested route:

Merchant support -> marketplace protection program -> payment dispute -> consumer protection complaint draft.

## Service Not Provided

Best first outputs:

- Demand for performance or refund.
- Timeline of missed appointment, outage, failure, or non-delivery.

Look for:

- Contract, service order, appointment confirmation.
- Proof user was available or complied with requirements.
- Company cancellation/no-show messages.

Suggested route:

Merchant support -> billing escalation -> payment dispute -> sector regulator if telecom, utility, insurance, transportation, or finance.

## Travel and Lodging

Best first outputs:

- Compensation/refund request to airline, hotel, booking platform, or host.
- Evidence packet: itinerary, cancellation notice, photos, receipts for replacement costs.

Look for:

- Booking terms and cancellation policy.
- Disruption reason and notice time.
- Receipts for reasonable mitigation expenses.
- Platform messages.

Suggested route:

Provider support -> booking platform -> card issuer/travel insurance -> relevant transportation or lodging complaint channel. Verify official agency rules before citing passenger rights.

## Telecom and Utilities

Best first outputs:

- Bill audit request.
- Charge table showing disputed line items.
- Cancellation or service-outage timeline.

Look for:

- Previous and current bills.
- Contract or promotional rate.
- Equipment return proof.
- Outage logs.

Suggested route:

Provider billing support -> retention/escalation team -> state public utility commission or telecom regulator draft -> payment dispute for clear billing errors.

## Complaint Draft Channel Selection

- FTC: Broad unfair/deceptive business practice patterns, scams, hard-to-cancel subscriptions. Complaints help enforcement; they usually do not resolve individual disputes directly.
- CFPB: U.S. consumer financial products: credit cards, bank accounts, credit reporting, debt collection, mortgages, loans, money transfers.
- State attorney general: State-level consumer protection complaints, especially when merchant operates in or sells to the user's state.
- BBB: Non-government business complaint mediation. Useful for response pressure, not a regulator.
- Sector regulator: Utilities, insurance, transportation, healthcare, telecom, education, or licensing boards.

Always prefer official portals and verify URLs in the current browser session when preparing a filing.
